DUAL-DIALOGUE: GENERAL BEST PRACTICES
Dual-dialogue (two characters speaking simultaneously, both lines printed side-by-side) is a powerful but easily overused tool. Here's when and how to deploy it:

WHEN TO USE IT
1. Conflict/Argument Escalation Characters talking over each other, neither listening. Creates tension and establishes power dynamics.

CHARACTER A          CHARACTER B
I'm not listening    You never do, you just—
to this again.       decide everything for us.
✓ Use when: Emotional beats escalate; people interrupt each other; simultaneous realization

2. Emotional Mirroring Two characters experiencing the same moment differently; their simultaneous dialogue reveals character contrast.

OPTIMIST             PESSIMIST
We can fix this.     We're already broken.
✓ Use when: Thematic resonance; character philosophy clash; parallel emotional journeys

3. Chaos/Overlapping Scenes Multiple conversations or moments happening at once (interrogation room + observation room; phone call + action).

SAM (in room)        ATLAS (via earpiece)
He's lying about—    —the timeline. I see it.
✓ Use when: Urgency; synchronized action; showing collaboration under pressure

WHEN NOT TO USE IT
Simple back-and-forth dialogue (use standard alternating format)
More than 3 lines per side (becomes unreadable)
When clarity is critical (exposition, plot information—readers need focus)
Casually or frequently (loses impact; feels gimmicky)
TECHNICAL RULES
Format:

CHARACTER A          CHARACTER B
Dialogue here.       Dialogue here.

CHARACTER A          CHARACTER B
More dialogue.       More dialogue.
Readability:

Keep each line SHORT (under 12 words ideally)
Use only 1–3 exchanges max per scene
Ensure both voices are distinct (vocabulary, rhythm, syntax)
EMOTIONAL/THEMATIC POWER
Dual-dialogue works best when it reveals philosophical or emotional opposition that's hard to express any other way:

SAM                  ATLAS
The system failed    The system works fine—
her. That's on us.   we're the ones who failed.
This single moment shows their fundamental disagreement without either having to explain it.
